# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist

A practical safety guide for college students in India.

---

## General Online Safety

1. **Use strong, unique passwords for every account.**  
   A strong password is at least 12 characters long and contains a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols.  
   Use a password manager (like Bitwarden or Google Password Manager) to remember them.

2. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all important accounts.**  
   This includes your email, GitHub, LinkedIn, and any banking app. 2FA means that even if someone gets your password, they cannot access your account without the second code.

3. **Never click on suspicious links — even if they come from a known contact.**  
   If a friend sends you an unexpected link via WhatsApp or Instagram DM, call them to verify before clicking. Their account may have been hacked.

4. **Do not install apps from unknown sources (outside Play Store / App Store).**  
   Many scam apps disguise themselves as utility tools. Only download apps from official app stores and check reviews before installing.

5. **Lock your phone with a PIN or biometric — always.**  
   An unlocked phone in the wrong hands can lead to instant financial and data loss.

6. **Be careful what you share in online forms and surveys.**  
   Legitimate platforms never ask for your Aadhaar number, full bank account details, or UPI PIN in a form.

7. **Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive transactions.**
   Public networks at cafes, airports, or hotels are often unencrypted, making it easy for hackers to intercept your data. If you must access your bank or work accounts on the go, use a trusted VPN or switch to your mobile data.

8. **Keep your device's operating system and apps updated.**
   Software updates frequently contain critical security patches that fix newly discovered vulnerabilities. Ignoring these updates leaves your phone or computer unnecessarily exposed to known cyber threats.

---

## UPI & Financial Safety

9. **NEVER enter your UPI PIN to RECEIVE money.**  
   Your UPI PIN is only needed when you are SENDING money. If someone asks you to enter your PIN to "accept" a payment, it is FRAUD. Stop immediately.

10. **Always verify the UPI collect request before accepting.**  
   When you receive a collect/payment request in your UPI app, check the sender's name and amount carefully. Fraudsters send collect requests disguised as payment confirmations.

11. **Do not share UPI screenshots publicly on social media.**  
   Your UPI ID is visible in transaction screenshots and can be exploited.

12. **Set a daily transaction limit on your UPI app.**  
    Most apps (Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm) allow you to set transaction limits. Keeping this low reduces potential loss in case of fraud.

## Reporting Cybercrime in India

If you or someone you know becomes a victim of cybercrime:

- **National Cyber Crime Portal:** https://cybercrime.gov.in  
  File a complaint online — available 24×7.

- **Cyber Crime Helpline:** **1930** (24×7 phone helpline)  
  Call immediately after a financial fraud — quick reporting can sometimes freeze the fraudster's account before they withdraw.

- **Nearest Police Station:** File a written complaint (FIR) for serious incidents like identity theft or harassment.

---

*Remember: You are not at fault for being targeted. Report immediately — quick action saves money and protects others.*
