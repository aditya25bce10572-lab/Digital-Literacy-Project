# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud
**Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness | CSE0001 Digital Literacy**

---

## Overview

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the fastest-growing cybercrimes in India, 
particularly targeting students, first-time smartphone users, and small business owners 
who use apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm, and BHIM.

---

## Case Study: "The Fake Buyer Scam" (Realistic Fictional Scenario)

Ravi, a second-year engineering student in Bhopal, posted his old laptop for sale on 
OLX. Within hours, a buyer contacted him via WhatsApp, expressing strong interest. The 
"buyer" claimed to be an Army officer stationed far away and said he would pay via 
Google Pay. He asked Ravi to share his UPI ID.

Ravi shared it. The buyer then sent a screenshot of a "payment of Rs. 25,000" and asked 
Ravi to "accept" it via a UPI request link sent to his phone. When Ravi clicked "Accept" 
and entered his UPI PIN, Rs. 8,500 was instantly debited from his account — not credited.

**What had happened:** The buyer had sent a COLLECT REQUEST, not a payment. In UPI, 
entering your PIN on a collect request authorises money to go OUT of your account. The 
buyer exploited the fact that Ravi did not understand this distinction.

---

## How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

1. Fraudster contacts victim via OLX, Facebook Marketplace, or WhatsApp.
2. Fraudster builds trust by posing as a professional (Army officer, NRI, etc.).
3. Fraudster sends a UPI COLLECT REQUEST disguised as a "payment confirmation."
4. Victim enters their UPI PIN, believing they are receiving money.
5. Money is instantly transferred to the fraudster's account.
6. Fraudster blocks the victim immediately.

---

## Who Is Typically Targeted

- Students and young adults selling items on second-hand marketplaces.
- First-time UPI users unfamiliar with the difference between "Send" and "Collect".
- People in urgent need of money who don't pause to verify.

---

## Consequences

- Immediate financial loss (often between Rs. 2,000 – Rs. 50,000).
- Psychological distress and loss of trust in digital payment systems.
- Difficulty recovering funds — transactions are often irreversible.
- Victims sometimes face blame for "negligence," adding to mental health impact.
