# Task 1 – Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic

## Tool Used
**Canva** (https://www.canva.com) – Free online graphic design platform

## What the Infographic Shows
The infographic is a single-page visual titled **"Your Digital Toolkit: A Guide for First-Year Students"**.  
It covers the following three topics:
1. **What is Digital Literacy?** – A brief definition with icons showing skills like online communication, safe browsing, and information evaluation.
2. **Safe Internet Practices** – Tips such as using strong passwords, not sharing OTPs, and identifying phishing links.
3. **Professional Online Presence** – How to set up a LinkedIn and GitHub profile, and what NOT to post publicly.

## Reflection
> *(Replace this with your own 150–200 word write-up after making your infographic on Canva)*

I used Canva to create my infographic because it offers ready-made templates that are easy to customise, even for someone with no design experience. I chose a clean, blue-and-white colour scheme to make the content easy to read at a glance. The hardest part was deciding what to include — there was so much information about digital literacy that narrowing it down to just three topics within one page was challenging. I eventually focused on what I felt was most immediately useful for a first-year student: understanding what digital literacy means, how to stay safe online, and how to start building a professional presence. One thing I found particularly interesting was learning how to use Canva's grid system to align elements neatly. I was surprised by how much visual design affects how seriously people take the content — a well-organised infographic feels more trustworthy than a cluttered one. This task taught me that communication is not just about what you say, but how you present it visually.
