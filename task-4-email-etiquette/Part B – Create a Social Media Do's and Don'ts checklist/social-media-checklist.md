# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

A responsible use checklist for maintaining a safe and professional digital presence.

---

## ✅ DO'S

1. **Keep your privacy settings updated.**  
   Regularly review who can see your posts, stories, and personal information on all platforms.

2. **Think before you post.**  
   Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor, parent, or future employer saw this?" If not, don't post it.

3. **Use a professional profile photo on LinkedIn and GitHub.**  
   A clear, well-lit photo with a neutral background makes a strong first impression.

4. **Give credit when sharing others' content.**  
   Tag the original creator or mention the source when reposting images, quotes, or articles.

5. **Report harassment, hate speech, and misinformation.**  
   Use platform reporting tools to flag harmful content — you are contributing to a safer internet.

6. **Separate personal and professional accounts where needed.**  
   Consider maintaining a professional LinkedIn while keeping personal social media accounts private.

7. **Verify information before sharing.**  
   Fake news spreads quickly. Cross-check facts using reliable sources before forwarding or sharing.

8. **Engage constructively in online discussions.**  
   Disagree respectfully. Healthy debate is fine; personal attacks and trolling are not.

---

## ❌ DON'TS

1. **Don't share your OTP, passwords, or bank details on any platform — ever.**  
   No legitimate company, bank, or authority will ask for these details via social media.

2. **Don't post your location in real time.**  
   Sharing live location (e.g., "Currently at XYZ") is a security and safety risk.

3. **Don't accept friend/connection requests from strangers indiscriminately.**  
   Fake profiles are commonly used for phishing, scamming, and data harvesting.

4. **Don't use social media to vent about your college, professors, or classmates.**  
   Even if your account is "private," screenshots can be taken and shared. Reputational damage is hard to undo.

5. **Don't click on suspicious links, even if shared by a known contact.**  
   Accounts can be hacked. Verify with the person before clicking links sent via DM.

6. **Don't participate in online challenges that involve personal risk.**  
   Many viral challenges have caused serious physical or mental harm — exercise judgement.

7. **Don't plagiarise or pass off others' creative work as your own.**  
   This applies to art, writing, photography, and code. It is both unethical and potentially illegal.
